The Panorama Route

The panorama route is a famous route and is very well signposted, so you can do it yourself; or there is also the option of guided tours that are run by local experts who can tell you all about the local flora and fauna. Below is a rough guide on how to see almost everything in one day, if you are going to drive yourself. To begin, you will enter the Blyde River Canyon Nature Reserve and then follow the signs to “the Three Rondavels” look-out point. There is a small fee to enter (about R200 per vehicle) and there is ample parking when you reach the top. There are also some market stalls, toilets, cafes etc., so you can spend if you want there.

Blyde River Canyon

Blyde river canyon is one of the most beautiful natural landmarks of South Africa. It is also one of the largest canyons in the world; Due to its proximity to several main entrances of Kruger National Park, Blyde River Canyon Nature Reserve and the whole Panorama Route make a very popular day trip in the area.

The Three Rondavels

The Three Rondavels, which are also known as “the Three Sisters,” are rock formations which are shaped like traditional African beehive huts, the Three Rondavels form three huge pinnacles of rock rising above the canyon below. The 3 rondavels give a spectacular view over the Blyde River Canyon. The entrance fee to the Three Rondavels entrance fee is R20 per person.

Bourke's Luck Potholes

The result of decades of swirling eddies of water where the Treur River meets the Blyde River, the tumult of which has caused extensive water erosion over time. The result is a series of cylindrical rock sculptures that look as though they would be more comfortable on the moon.The potholes are located 35 km north of Graskop on the R532 road. Entrance fee is R60 for an adult and R20 per child and free to children under 6 years old. You will be pleased to know that there are braai and picnic facilities!
